# Syllabus Control Document: Wellness Pillars & Resources

This document defines the conceptual framework of the NSCC Student Wellness App, the "Pillars", and the supporting evidence for wording choices.

## 1. The Four Core Pillars

The application tracks four key dimensions of wellness, chosen for their direct impact on student success and retention.

### A. Sleep Quality
- **Wording**: "Sleep Quality" instead of "Hours Slept".
- **Why**: Focuses on the *restorative* aspect. Research shows that perceived sleep quality is Often more predictive of cognitive function than duration alone.
- **Institutional Context**: High correlation between sleep hygiene and GPA at NSCC.

### B. Stress Balance
- **Wording**: "Stress Balance" instead of "Stress Level".
- **Why**: Acknowledges that stress is inevitable but manageable. The goal is "balance", not zero stress.
- **Cited Resource**: NSCC Student Health Plan (Advising & Counselling).

### C. Cognitive Capacity
- **Wording**: "Cognitive Capacity" (Internal label: BRAIN).
- **Why**: Focuses on attention, memory, and the ability to manage tasks, which can be significantly reduced by stress and fatigue.
- **Behavioral Indicators**: Ability to attend class, complete assignments, and engage socially.

### D. Social Belonging
- **Wording**: "Social Belonging" instead of "Social Life".
- **Why**: Higher-level psychological need. Belonging is a primary predictor of student retention.
- **Cited Resource**: NSCC Student Association (SA) & Affinity Groups.

## 2. Vocabulary & Tone

- **Supportive**: Uses phrases like "You are not alone" and "Be gentle with yourself".
- **Professional**: Aligns with NSCC corporate branding (NSCC Blue, Sage-Bark color palette).
- **Confidential**: Emphasizes that "This entry is visible only to you" to build trust.
